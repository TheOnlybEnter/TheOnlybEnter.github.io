About Matt

My name is Matt. I'm a student at Allen HighSchool and am currently enrolled in computer science.
I've have many reasons for my interest in Computer Science but my one of my major interest first began with Call of Duty.
Although I know how some AAA games work there is still a vast amount of information I have yet to know. I watched a small youtuber who goes by JohnnyJ25 who would every once in a while make videos about Call Of Duty.
One topic he broguht out was Call of Duties Zombie Mode which contains a Round change. To start off zombie's health is represented by a 32-bit integers which is between 2,147,483,647 and -2,147,483,647. Health continously gets larger as rounds go by, but thats not the case after round 163. This is value exceeds on round 163 hence the first Insta Kill Round (a term coined by the Zombies Community, after zombie health is bugged). If the result of an arithmetic operation falls outside the 32-bit integral it causes overflow. Now this does not mean that overflow is the issue but rather the produced side affect of a negative value is. Note - that since it can be both positive and negative, insta kill rounds wont always happen. So with this negative value, the health is so low that you could one shot the zombie with the starting pistol. This was of course true for CoD's before2015. In recent CoD games zombie health is now represented by a 64-bit integer, and the rounds are capped at 255 to stop the previous issue of negative values being produced. Overall, interesting things like this is what got me into Computer Science.
